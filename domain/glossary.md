# Glossary

Trading and options terminology used in InMoney.

## General Trading Terms

### Ask
The price at which a seller is willing to sell a security.

### Bid
The price at which a buyer is willing to buy a security.

### Spread (Bid-Ask)
The difference between the bid and ask prices.

### Volume
The number of shares or contracts traded during a period.

### Market Cap
Total market value of a company's outstanding shares.

### Ticker Symbol
Abbreviated identifier for a publicly traded company (e.g., AAPL for Apple).

---

## Options Terminology

### Option
A contract giving the right (but not obligation) to buy or sell an asset at a specified price.

### Call Option
Gives the holder the right to BUY the underlying asset.

### Put Option
Gives the holder the right to SELL the underlying asset.

### Strike Price
The price at which the option can be exercised.

### Expiration Date
The date when the option contract expires.

### Premium
The price paid to purchase an option.

### Exercise
Using the option to buy or sell the underlying asset.

### Assignment
Being required to fulfill the obligation of a short option position.

---

## Option Status

### In The Money (ITM)
- **Call**: Strike price < Current stock price
- **Put**: Strike price > Current stock price

### At The Money (ATM)
Strike price ≈ Current stock price

### Out of The Money (OTM)
- **Call**: Strike price > Current stock price
- **Put**: Strike price < Current stock price

---

## Greeks

### Delta (Δ)
Rate of change of option price relative to underlying price.
- Calls: 0 to 1
- Puts: -1 to 0

### Gamma (Γ)
Rate of change of Delta relative to underlying price.

### Theta (Θ)
Rate of time decay; how much value the option loses per day.

### Vega (ν)
Sensitivity to implied volatility changes.

### Rho (ρ)
Sensitivity to interest rate changes.

---

## Volatility

### Implied Volatility (IV)
Market's expectation of future volatility, derived from option prices.

### Historical Volatility (HV)
Actual volatility based on past price movements.

### IV Rank
Current IV compared to its range over a period (typically 52 weeks).

### IV Percentile
Percentage of days IV was lower than current IV.

---

## Options Strategies

### Covered Call
Long stock + Short call

### Protective Put
Long stock + Long put

### Vertical Spread
Two options of same type, different strikes, same expiration.

### Iron Condor
Short strangle + Long strangle (further OTM)

### Straddle
Long call + Long put at same strike

### Strangle
Long call + Long put at different strikes

---

## Order Types

### Market Order
Execute immediately at current market price.

### Limit Order
Execute at specified price or better.

### Stop Order
Becomes market order when price reaches trigger.

### Stop-Limit Order
Becomes limit order when price reaches trigger.
